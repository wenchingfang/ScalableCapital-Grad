Why investing, banking, and digital products

From startup challenges to practical digital solutions

Co-founding my own patisserie startup taught me an early lesson: technology is only meaningful when it solves real problems. When the COVID-19 pandemic hit Taiwan shortly after our launch, we had to adapt quickly from a physical storefront to an online delivery model. To manage the sudden increase in digital inquiries, I built and deployed an automated customer service chatbot on LINE.

Through that experience, I learned what users and business owners truly value in technology: not flashy features, but reliable, intuitive tools that reduce friction and make daily decisions easier. That is also what attracts me to digital investing products. When finance becomes simple, transparent, and accessible, more people can participate with confidence.

I carried the same approach into my internship at Crescendo Lab, where I helped build the digital infrastructure for a B2B tech product launch. By integrating Google Analytics to track user behavior and refining our omnichannel strategy, we engaged more than 400 brands and exceeded our launch target by 25%. Whether I am solving a bottleneck for a small business or evaluating digital performance for hundreds of B2B clients, I am driven by the same objective: creating digital solutions with measurable, practical impact.

Investment products need trust, not just growth

Scalable Capital’s mission resonates with me because investing is both personal and long-term. People are not only trusting a platform with money; they are trusting it with future goals, security, and choice.

My Risk Management background taught me to look at innovation through a safety-first lens. During my internship in portfolio risk governance, I learned how controls, transparency, and data-driven decisions protect both clients and institutions. This is the perspective I would bring to a digital bank: build for scale, but never lose sight of responsibility.

Why the Investments & Banking Graduate Program

Business rotations: understanding how value is built

I am especially excited by the opportunity to rotate through business functions such as Product, Finance, Operations, Marketing, or the CEO Office. My experience has taught me to connect customer needs, data, and execution. At Scalable Capital, I would like to deepen that perspective in the context of investing and banking products used by clients across Europe.

Control rotations: understanding how trust is protected

The program’s control-function rotations are equally important to me. Areas such as Risk Management, AML, and Compliance are not separate from innovation; they make sustainable innovation possible. My academic focus in Risk Management and my practical experience with responsible AI training have shaped how I think: move quickly, but with clarity, evidence, and safeguards.

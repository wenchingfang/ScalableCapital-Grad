02 · Why Scalable?

Making investing simpler, without compromising trust

Using technology to make finance easier

During my internship at BNP Paribas, I gained exposure to securities services and the post-trade processes that support institutional clients after execution. I also worked on a Power BI dashboard that brought different data sources together and made financial and operational performance easier to track.

That experience showed me how technology can simplify complex financial processes and save time for future users. This is one reason Scalable Capital attracts me: it applies the same principle to investing, using technology to make trading and ETF savings plans easier to access and understand.

Building for the future of European investing

The European financial landscape is changing quickly, with more clients expecting digital access, transparent products, and greater freedom of choice. I am excited by Scalable Capital’s role in this shift as a digital investment and banking platform with brokerage, ETF portfolios, banking services, crypto access, and B2B partnerships.

The Graduate Program feels especially relevant to me because it combines business and control functions. I want to learn how modern investment and banking products are built, operated, monitored, and scaled in a regulated environment — and contribute to a platform that is shaping the future of investing in Europe.

03 · What I bring

Analytical finance experience with a digital execution mindset

Turning financial complexity into clarity

At DWS, I strengthened my quantitative skills through portfolio risk governance. I worked with risk indicators such as VaR, tracking error, and so on, which gave me a deep understanding of how financial instruments are assessed and controlled.

Beyond the analysis , I learned to communicate risk clearly to different stakeholders. This experience shaped the way I approach financial topics: with attention to detail, data-driven thinking, and the ability to translate complex information into practical insights.

Owning execution from idea to outcome

My startup experience taught me to take responsibility when conditions change. When COVID-19 affected my patisserie business in Taipei, we shifted to an online model, and I built a LINE chatbot to manage customer inquiries more efficiently.

I later brought the same hands-on approach into the tech-startup sector, where I supported a B2B product launch that engaged 400+ brands and exceeded its launch target by 25%. These experiences taught me to stay close to users, move from problem to solution quickly, and follow through until ideas become measurable results.
